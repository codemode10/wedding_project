Travel in Provence

Here are a some villages you cannot skip when in Provence.

Les Baux-de-Provence

Located in the Alpilles mountains, atop a rocky outcrop that is crowned with a ruined castle overlooking the plains to the south. Little far away but stunning. Poplation 391.

Lourmarin

Listed as "one of the most beautiful villages in France", Lourmarin nestles in the middle of vineyards, olive groves and almond trees. Lourmarin has been settled for at least a thousand years, and was a Neolithic campsite before that. A fortress was first built at the site in the 12th century, and was rebuilt by Foulques d'Agoult in the 15th century on the foundations of the earlier castle. Close to Mathilde's house

Beautiful 12th centure church ontop of a hill in a stunning little village. This one is one of my favourite villages in the area.

Aix-en-Provence

Aix-en-Provence is the capital of the region and a university town in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region of southern France. It was the birthplace of Post-Impressionist painter Paul Cézanne. Try and visit Aix during the saturday markets.

Open maps

Marseille

Marseille, a port city, is Frances' 2nd largest city. It has been the crossroads of immigration and trade since its founding by the Greeks circa 600 B.C. The best two areas are Le Panier and Vallon des Auffes. Le Panier is a beautiful old artsy area with great resturants and bars. Vallon des Auffes is an amazing place to go for a sunset drink or to eat in the few resturants lined along the shore.

About French Weddings

Weddings in Frace are sligtly different to those in Australia. In France you can only legally be married in your local town hall. Some couples chose to do this part before the wedding day and some chose to do this the morning of the wedding day with just a few people in attendence. France is quite a religious coutry and marriage celebrants are not common as they hold no legal rights. So we have chosen to do the town hall part last year with the wedding this September.
