About French Weddings

Nuptial traditions vary across the globe, with French weddings presenting a distinctive approach compared to English ones. In France, matrimonial proceedings are a two-part affair: a civil ceremony and a religious one. The civil ceremony is a legal requirement, conducted exclusively at the town hall - the only venue in France where marriage can be lawfully officiated. Unlike in Australia and some other countries, marriage celebrants do not have any legal jurisdiction in France.

Couples have the freedom to arrange their civil ceremony on the same day as their religious one or at an earlier date. In our case, we chose to conduct this civil ceremony last November in Paris. Following this, the religious ceremony serves as the primary celebration of the marriage, where customary rituals, including the reception, occur. We are delighted to announce that this part of the wedding is scheduled for September 15th.
