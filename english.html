About French Weddings

Weddings in France are slightly different to English weddings. In France, weddings comprise of two parts, the civil and the religious ceremony. The civil part takes place in the town hall, which is the only place you can be legally married in France. Marriage celebrants are not a thing here as they hold no legal powers like they do in Australia etc. Couples can chose to do the civil part the morning of or months before, as we did last November in Paris. The second part, the religious ceremony is the main wedding where the usual traditions take place including the reception, and this will be the part taking place on September 15th.
